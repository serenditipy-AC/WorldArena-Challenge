Challenge Overview

The WorldArena Challenge aims to establish a standardized evaluation protocol for embodied world models, focusing on both perceptual fidelity and functional utility.

World models have recently emerged as a key paradigm for embodied intelligence, enabling agents to simulate future environments through video generation and latent dynamics modeling. However, existing evaluation protocols remain largely limited to visual quality metrics, failing to capture whether generated environments are actually usable for downstream decision-making.

To address this limitation, WorldArena introduces a unified benchmark that evaluates models along two tracks as follows:

  • Track 1: Video Perception Quality — evaluating visual quality, motion quality, content consistency, physics adherence, 3D accuracy, and controllability.
  • Track 2: Embodied Task Functionality — whether generated worlds support action planning and policy training/evaluation.

This challenge provides a platform to benchmark models under a holistic evaluation paradigm, encouraging progress toward functional world simulators rather than purely visual generators.
